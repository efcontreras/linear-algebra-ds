Spectral Theory of Linear Operators

1 Motivation

Spectral theory studies how a linear operator acts by decomposing it into independent directions that are scaled.

In finite-dimensional real vector spaces, this means understanding:

  • eigenvalues,
  • eigenvectors,
  • diagonalization,
  • orthogonal decompositions.

In Data Science, spectral theory underlies: - PCA, - spectral clustering, - graph Laplacians, - low-rank approximations.

2 Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Definition (Eigenvalue and eigenvector)

Let \(T:V\to V\) be a linear operator on a vector space \(V\). A scalar \(\lambda\in\mathbb{R}\) is an eigenvalue of \(T\) if there exists a nonzero vector \(v\in V\) such that

\[ T(v) = \lambda v. \]

The vector \(v\) is called an eigenvector associated with \(\lambda\).

For a matrix \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\), this reads:

\[ Av = \lambda v. \]

3 The Characteristic Polynomial

Definition (Characteristic polynomial)

The characteristic polynomial of \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\) is

\[ p_A(\lambda) := \det(A - \lambda I). \]

Eigenvalues are the roots of \(p_A\).

4 Algebraic vs. Geometric Multiplicity

Definition

Let \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of \(A\).

  • Its algebraic multiplicity is its multiplicity as a root of \(p_A\).
  • Its geometric multiplicity is \[ \dim \ker(A - \lambda I). \]

Always: \[ 1 \le \text{geometric multiplicity} \le \text{algebraic multiplicity}. \]

5 Diagonalization

Definition (Diagonalizable matrix)

A matrix \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\) is diagonalizable if there exists an invertible matrix \(P\) such that

\[ A = P D P^{-1}, \]

where \(D\) is diagonal.

Diagonal entries of \(D\) are eigenvalues of \(A\).

6 Why Diagonalization Matters

If \(A = P D P^{-1}\), then:

  • powers: \(A^k = P D^k P^{-1}\)
  • exponentials: \(e^{tA} = P e^{tD} P^{-1}\)
  • dynamics decouple into independent modes

This is why spectral theory is fundamental in: - linear dynamical systems, - diffusion processes, - graph methods.

7 Self-Adjoint (Symmetric) Operators

Definition (Self-adjoint operator)

A linear operator \(T:V\to V\) on an inner product space is self-adjoint if

\[ \langle T u, v \rangle = \langle u, T v \rangle \quad \text{for all } u,v\in V. \]

In matrix form (Euclidean inner product):

\[ A = A^\top. \]

8 The Spectral Theorem (Finite-Dimensional)

Theorem (Spectral Theorem)

Let \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\) be symmetric. Then:

  1. All eigenvalues of \(A\) are real.
  2. Eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal.
  3. There exists an orthogonal matrix \(Q\) such that

\[ A = Q \Lambda Q^\top, \]

where \(\Lambda\) is diagonal.

Proof idea (click to expand)

Proof.

Proof (idea)

The proof uses: - the fact that symmetric matrices define real quadratic forms, - maximization of the Rayleigh quotient on the unit sphere, - induction on dimension.

Orthogonality follows from symmetry: if \(Av=\lambda v\) and \(Aw=\mu w\) with \(\lambda\neq\mu\), then

\[ \lambda \langle v,w\rangle = \langle Av,w\rangle = \langle v,Aw\rangle = \mu \langle v,w\rangle, \]

so \(\langle v,w\rangle=0\). □

9 Quadratic Forms and Rayleigh Quotient

Definition (Rayleigh quotient)

For a symmetric matrix \(A\) and nonzero \(x\):

\[ R_A(x) := \frac{x^\top A x}{x^\top x}. \]

Theorem

The maximum and minimum values of \(R_A(x)\) over \(x\neq 0\) are the largest and smallest eigenvalues of \(A\).

This result is the variational foundation of PCA.

10 Computational Example (Python)

import numpy as np

A = np.array([[2, 1],
              [1, 3]])

eigvals, eigvecs = np.linalg.eigh(A)
eigvals, eigvecs
  • np.linalg.eigh exploits symmetry.
  • Eigenvectors returned are orthonormal.

11 Geometry of Spectral Decomposition

The decomposition

\[ A = \sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i\, v_i v_i^\top \]

shows that a symmetric linear operator acts as: - projection onto the eigenvector \(v_i\), - followed by scaling by the eigenvalue \(\lambda_i\).

This representation expresses \(A\) as a sum of rank-1 operators.

12 Data Science Connections

12.1 Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

Given a centered data matrix \(X \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}\), the empirical covariance matrix is

\[ \mathrm{Cov}(X) = \frac{1}{m} X^\top X, \]

which is symmetric and positive semidefinite.

PCA consists of computing the eigen-decomposition of \(\mathrm{Cov}(X)\) and selecting the eigenvectors associated with the largest eigenvalues.

12.2 Spectral Clustering

In spectral clustering, data points are viewed as nodes of a graph. The (normalized) graph Laplacian is a symmetric matrix.

Clustering is performed by embedding nodes using eigenvectors associated with the smallest nonzero eigenvalues of the Laplacian.

12.3 Optimization and Conditioning

Quadratic objectives of the form

\[ f(x) = x^\top A x \]

are governed by the spectrum of \(A\):

  • \(f\) is convex if and only if \(A \succeq 0\),
  • the conditioning of the problem is controlled by the ratio \(\lambda_{\max} / \lambda_{\min}\).

13 Summary

  • Symmetric matrices admit orthogonal diagonalization.
  • Spectral decomposition expresses operators as sums of rank-1 projections.
  • PCA and spectral clustering are direct consequences of spectral theory.
  • Optimization behavior is dictated by eigenvalues.

14 Preview: Singular Value Decomposition

Not all matrices are symmetric. The Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) extends spectral ideas to arbitrary rectangular matrices and is the central decomposition in modern Data Science.
